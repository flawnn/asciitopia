# Attribution

asciitopia's patterns draw on the work of some wonderful terminal-art projects.
As of v0.1, asciitopia contains **no code or art copied from these projects** —
they are credited below as inspiration, which carries our gratitude but no
license relationship.

## cbonsai — John Allbritten (jallbrit)
https://gitlab.com/jallbrit/cbonsai (GPL-3.0)

A future bonsai pattern's growth aesthetic — branching trunks, side shoots,
and leafy die-off — will be inspired by cbonsai's beautiful procedural trees.
It is not part of the v0.1 release; when it lands, its growth weights,
characters, and pot art will be written independently (clean-room), not
ported from cbonsai's GPL-3.0 source.

## weathr — Veirt
https://github.com/veirt/weathr (GPL-3.0)

A future weather pattern's cozy scene — a house under living weather — will
be inspired by weathr's terminal weather scenes. It is not part of the v0.1
release; when it lands, its scene art and weather systems will be drawn and
implemented independently (clean-room), not ported from weathr's GPL-3.0
source. Cloud shapes specifically may instead be adapted from wego (see
below), which is MIT-licensed at the true origin of that art style.

## wego / wttr.in
